Executive Brief: TACV Quota Scenario Modeling Initiative
Purpose
This initiative creates a standardized analytical framework for evaluating customer capacity situations and translating them into actionable quota planning scenarios. The prompt serves as an LLM-powered analysis engine that processes account data to deliver two critical outputs:
Customer Options Analysis - Equipping sales teams with fact-based capacity management options they can discuss with customers
Quota Planning Scenarios - Providing finance/planning teams with bookable TACV projections across conservative, base, and stretch cases
Business Problem
Snowflake's consumption-based model creates complexity in quota planning:
Timing Ambiguity: Customers can pay overages, pull forward capacity, or amend contracts - each with different TACV implications
Bookability Rules: Not all revenue events count toward quota (e.g., pull-forwards are re-timing, not new capacity)
Multi-Segment Contracts: Complex deal structures with shortened periods and exhausted future years require nuanced analysis
Planning Confidence: Leadership needs reliable floor/upside scenarios, not single-point estimates
Without standardized analysis, quota planning becomes inconsistent and sales conversations lack data-driven options.
What This Accomplishes
For Planning Teams
Scenario-Based Forecasting: Conservative (floor), Base (most likely), and Stretch (upside) TACV projections with confidence levels
Bookability Clarity: Clear marking of which revenue counts toward quota vs. re-timing
Risk Visibility: Data quality flags and key assumptions documented upfront
For Sales Teams
Customer Conversation Toolkit: Pre-calculated options with capacity sufficiency math
Trade-off Analysis: Each option shows implications for timing, costs, and future flexibility
Capacity Planning: Precise calculations on when customers will hit overage again based on burn rates
Process Efficiency
Standardized Analysis: Eliminates inconsistent ad-hoc spreadsheets
Scalability: Can process hundreds of accounts through LLM automation
Time Savings: Reduces 30-60 min manual analyses to seconds
Technical Approach
The prompt functions as a decision tree that:
Analyzes contract structure (single vs. multi-year, segment positioning)
Identifies confirmed renewal events in FY27 (overages, renewals)
Evaluates customer options with capacity sufficiency calculations
Determines TACV bookability for each option
Generates planning scenarios with confidence levels
The framework handles complex edge cases (final segments, negative capacity, downsells) that break traditional quota models.
